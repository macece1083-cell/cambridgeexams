Script and Candidate Materials

7-9 minutes
1 child
Part 1: Find the differences.
Part 2: Information exchange.
Part 3-4: Picture story and personal questions.
Topic focus: water projects, city streets, healthy habits, transport, holidays.
Opening script - read aloud

Hello. My name is _______. What's your name?

How old are you?

Thank you. Now we are going to look at some pictures.

Part 1 - Find the Differencesabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Here are two pictures. They look the same, but some things are different.

I am going to say something about my picture. You tell me how your picture is different.

Use two similar busy city street pictures.

Examiner saysExpected candidate response

In my picture, the boy is walking with a dog.In my picture, the boy is riding a bicycle.
In my picture, the bus is red.In my picture, the bus is green.
In my picture, the police officer is talking to a driver.In my picture, the police officer is stopping the cars.
In my picture, the bridge is grey.In my picture, the bridge is blue.
Part 2 - Information Exchangeabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now we are going to talk about two water projects.

I don't know about the Blue River Team, so I am going to ask you some questions. Then you ask me about Save Our Water.

Give the child the Blue River Team card. Keep the Save Our Water card.

Examiner asks candidate

  1. How many students are in the Blue River Team?
  2. Where do they collect water from?
  3. What do they use the water for?
  4. Which day do they meet?
  5. What is their next project?

Part 3 - Picture Storyabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now look at these pictures. They show a story. The story is called A Healthy Day In The City.

Look at the pictures first.

In picture one, Defne is filling her water bottle before school. It is a sunny morning.

Now you tell the story.

Picture 2Defne walks to the park and runs with her friend.
Picture 3She sees Kerem. He has a toothache and looks unhappy.
Picture 4Nurse Ayse helps Kerem. Later, the children drink water and eat vegetable soup.

Support prompts if needed

  • What is Defne carrying?
  • What is Kerem's problem?
  • Who helps him?
  • What healthy food do they eat?
Part 4 - Personal Questionsabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now let's talk about you.

  1. Do you live in a city or a town?
  2. What places are near your home?
  3. How do you come to school?
  4. What do you do to stay healthy?
  5. How much water do you drink every day?
  6. Where would you like to go on holiday?
  7. Do you prefer the beach, the mountains or a city?
  8. How can children help their city?
Closing script

Thank you. That is the end of the test.

Speaking Assessment Rubric

Practice classroom rubric for A2 Flyers speaking. Maximum score: 20. The shield estimate is for classroom feedback only.

Part531Score
Part 1: Find the differences Describes differences clearly using colour, position, number or action. Finds some differences with short answers and some support. Finds very few differences or needs frequent help.
Part 2: Information exchange Answers and asks simple questions accurately. Answers most questions; asks some questions with support. Has difficulty asking or answering information questions.
Part 3: Picture story Tells a clear short story using simple connected sentences. Describes pictures with basic sentences; needs some prompts. Uses isolated words or needs frequent prompting.
Part 4: Personal questions Answers personal questions clearly and adds simple details. Answers most questions with short but understandable responses. Gives very limited answers or often does not respond.
