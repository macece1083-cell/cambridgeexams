Flyers Speaking Exam
Exam 1

Examiner booklet for one child and one examiner. Topics match Flyers Exam 1: family, school, food, nature, picnic and classroom.

Practice material in a Cambridge-style layout. Use simple paired pictures or adapt the visuals from the prepared Flyers exam.

Script and Candidate Materials

7-9 minutes
1 child
Part 1: Find the differences.
Part 2: Information exchange.
Part 3-4: Picture story and personal questions.
Topic focus: family picnic, school information, food, nature, classroom objects.
Opening script - read aloud

Hello. My name is _______. What's your name?

How old are you?

Thank you. Now we are going to look at some pictures.

Part 1 - Find the Differencesabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Here are two pictures. They look the same, but some things are different.

I am going to say something about my picture. You tell me how your picture is different.

Use two similar park picnic pictures.

Examiner saysExpected candidate response
In my picture, the boy is eating a sandwich.In my picture, the boy is eating pasta.
In my picture, the grandmother is cooking in the kitchen.In my picture, the grandmother is watering flowers in the garden.
In my picture, the girl is reading under a tree.In my picture, the girl is taking photos of flowers.
In my picture, the backpack is on the chair.In my picture, the backpack is under the chair.
In my picture, the star on the wall is blue.In my picture, the star is yellow.
Part 2 - Information Exchangeabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now we are going to talk about two schools.

I don't know about Green Park School, so I am going to ask you some questions. Then you ask me about Sunshine International School.

Give the child the Green Park School card. Keep the Sunshine International School card.

Examiner asks candidate

  1. How many students are there at Green Park School?
  2. What is the favourite subject?
  3. What is the best friend's name?
  4. What time is lunch?
  5. What after-school club is there?

Candidate asks examiner

  2. What is the favourite subject?

Part 3 - Picture Storyabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now look at these pictures. They show a story. The story is called The Picnic Cake.

Look at the pictures first.

In picture one, Aunt Maria is carrying a big cake to the picnic blanket. The family are in the park and everyone is happy.

Now you tell the story.

Picture 2A boy runs near the blanket with a football. The cake begins to move.
Picture 3The dog runs to the cake and everyone looks surprised.
Picture 4The cake is safe. The family sit together and eat lunch.

Support prompts if needed

  • What is the boy doing?
  • Where is the dog?
  • How do the family feel?
  • What happens at the end?
Part 4 - Personal Questionsabout 2 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now let's talk about you.

  1. Who do you live with?
  2. What do you like doing with your family?
  3. What is your favourite food?
  4. What do you usually eat for lunch?
  5. Do you like studying science? Why / Why not?
  6. Do you like going to parks?
  7. What animals can you see near your home?
  8. What do you do after school?
Closing script

Thank you. That is the end of the test.

Speaking Assessment Rubric

Practice classroom rubric for A2 Flyers speaking. Maximum score: 20. The shield estimate is for classroom feedback only.

Part531Score
Part 1: Find the differences Describes differences clearly using colour, position, number or action. Finds some differences with short answers and some support. Finds very few differences or needs frequent help.
Part 2: Information exchange Answers and asks simple questions accurately. Answers most questions; asks some questions with support. Has difficulty asking or answering information questions.
Part 3: Picture story Tells a clear short story using simple connected sentences. Describes pictures with basic sentences; needs some prompts. Uses isolated words or needs frequent prompting.
Part 4: Personal questions Answers personal questions clearly and adds simple details. Answers most questions with short but understandable responses. Gives very limited answers or often does not respond.
