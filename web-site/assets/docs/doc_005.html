A2 Key for Schools - Practice Speaking

KET Speaking Exam
Test 2

Examiner booklet for one student and one examiner. Topics match KET Test 2: gadgets, markets, recycled fashion, history, music and weather.

Practice adaptation: official A2 Key speaking is normally taken with two candidates. This version is designed for one-to-one classroom testing.
Examiner copy

Script and Materials

8-10 minutes
1 student
Part 1: Interview and personal information.
Part 2: Individual talk with examiner follow-up.
Materials: One prompt card for Part 2.
Topic focus: old and new gadgets, markets, fashion, history projects, music, photos and weather.
Opening script - read aloud

Good morning / afternoon. My name is _______.

What is your name? And what is your surname?

How old are you? Thank you.

Part 1 - Interview4 minutes
Interlocutor says

First, I am going to ask you some questions about yourself.

Ask 6-8 questions. Use follow-up questions when the answer is short.

Main questions

  1. Do you like going to markets? Why / Why not?
  2. What do you usually buy with your pocket money?
  3. Do you use a tablet or computer for school?
  4. What gadget do you use most?
  5. What kind of music do you like?
  6. What clothes do you like wearing?
  7. Do you like learning about the past?
  8. What do you do when the weather is rainy?

Follow-up prompts

  • Tell me more about that.
  • Why is it useful?
  • How often do you use it?
  • Who helps you with that?
  • Would you like to try something different?
Part 2 - Individual Talk and Discussion4-6 minutes
Interlocutor says

Now I am going to give you some ideas. Your school wants to organise an Old and New Day.

Please look at the ideas. First, tell me which activities you like and why. Then choose the best idea for your school.

Give the student the prompt card. Let the student speak for about one minute. Then ask follow-up questions.

  • a recycled fashion show
  • a poster about old gadgets
  • a visit to a history museum
  • a music performance with old and new sounds
  • an old photograph exhibition

Examiner follow-up questions

  1. Which activity would be easiest to prepare?
  2. Which activity would be the most fun?
  3. What could students learn from old photographs?
  4. Would you like to present your project to the class?
  5. Is it better to use old things or buy new things?

Useful candidate language

  • This sounds fun because ...
  • I don't think this is easy because ...
  • I would choose ...
  • I prefer ... because ...
  • Students can learn about ...
Closing script

Thank you. That is the end of the test.

Speaking Assessment Rubric

Practice classroom rubric for the one-student KET speaking adaptation. Maximum score: 20.

Criterion531Score
Grammar and vocabulary Uses simple A2 grammar and topic vocabulary clearly; errors do not stop communication. Uses basic phrases; some errors or repeated words, but meaning is usually clear. Very limited language; frequent errors make meaning difficult.
Pronunciation Generally clear; word stress and sounds are easy to understand. Mostly understandable; examiner sometimes needs repetition. Often unclear; examiner needs frequent repetition.
Interaction with examiner Answers naturally, responds to follow-up questions, and keeps the conversation going. Answers questions but needs some support or prompting. Gives very short answers and needs a lot of support.
Task achievement Gives relevant answers, simple reasons, and chooses an activity with explanation. Completes most of the task with short reasons. Completes only small parts of the task.
